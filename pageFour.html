A Note on How to Use the STREAMING API

Now you may be wondering what is this STREAMING API and how does it differ from what we have already been using. Think of the STREAMING API as a continuous request rather than a one time request as we have been doing. We are essentially communicating with Twitter's servers continuously and are asking for updates. As long as there is a connection, specifically an open HTTPS connection between your computer and their servers, Twitter will continously monitor for "events". What this means is that our bot can now interact or respond to certain "events". "Events" are for example user interaction with our bot and result in our bot taking actions such as responding to people when they follow or mention you. There are many other aspects and things we can do but in this example we are only going to simply look at issuing a response for when someone follows you.

When this bot is running, it will randomly select a car company name, convert it to binary, and post that conversion as a question. 30 seconds later, the bot will tweet the correct answer right after. It should be noted that you should limit the number of times your bot can tweet in a certain amount of time. Twitter is not to fond of bots that can tweet at ridiculous speeds. In our example above, if the timer were not in place, both tweets would be sent essentially simultaneously. It is recommended that you limit the speed at which you bot posts multiple tweets as abusing the system can result in termination of your bot and of your account.
